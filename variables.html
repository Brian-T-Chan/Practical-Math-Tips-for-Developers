Variable Substitutions

Often there are questions such as the following.

Given
\[ y = x^2 + 4x + 7 \;\;\;\;\;\;\; (1) \]
and
\[ x = \frac{1}{5u - 4} \;\;\;\;\;\;\;\;\;\;\;\;\; (2) \]
Substitute the right side of Equation (2) for the variable \(x\) in Equation (1).

We can define two functions, one representing Equation (1) and one representing Equation (2).

var y = x =>

x ** 2 + 4 * x + 7;

var x = u =>

3 / (5 * u - 4);

Now define

var f = u => y(x(u));

The reason we want to, in the above equation, substitute the right side of the formula in Equation (2) for the variable \(x\) in Equation (1) is because we want to determine f with an equation.
